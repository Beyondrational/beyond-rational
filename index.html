What we make

We make acoustic surfaces. Compressed wood fibre with nothing else added, specified for ceilings and walls where a room can't be allowed to sound like a recording studio. Our first product, Aisti, ships from Helsinki.

How we work

Foam-formed,
not pressed.

We adapted our process from the Finnish paper industry. Instead of pressing fibre flat under load, we suspend it in foam — the fibres set into an open cell structure that absorbs sound at the cell level, without binders or plastics.

It is a slow process. It is also the only one we know that produces a single-substance acoustic plate. Material first, all the way through.
